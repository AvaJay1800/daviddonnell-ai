Claude Prompt Pack for Small Businesses

Claude Prompt Pack for Small Businesses

10 copy-paste prompts to help you get real work done with AI, starting today. No technical setup required. Just open Claude at claude.ai, paste the prompt, fill in the brackets, and you are ready to go.

How to use this pack: Go to claude.ai (free account) or any AI chat tool. Copy a prompt below, paste it into the chat, replace anything in [brackets] with your real details, and hit send. That is it. No tools to install, no accounts to set up beyond Claude itself.
1
Write a Quote or Estimate Follow-Up Email
Use after a site visit or inquiry to follow up on a quote you gave.
Copy and paste this prompt
Write a professional, friendly email to a potential customer following up on a quote I gave them for [describe the job]. The estimate was $[amount]. I want to sound confident but not pushy. Keep it under 150 words.
2
Respond to a Negative Review
Use when a bad review appears on Google or Yelp.
Copy and paste this prompt
A customer left this review: [paste review]. Write a professional, calm response that acknowledges their concern, briefly explains what happened (I will fill in the details), and invites them to contact me directly to make it right. Keep it under 100 words.
3
Write a Job Post for a Trade or Service Role
Use when hiring a technician, assistant, or crew member.
Copy and paste this prompt
Write a job post for a [job title] position at my small [industry] business in [city]. We need someone with [key skills]. The pay is [range]. Write it in a straightforward, no-nonsense tone that will appeal to experienced tradespeople. Include what the day-to-day looks like and what we offer.
4
Follow Up After Completing a Job
Use to ask for a review or referral after finishing work for a customer.
Copy and paste this prompt
Write a short, friendly follow-up message to send to a customer after I just completed a [type of job] for them. I want to thank them, make sure they are happy, and politely ask them to leave a Google review if they have a minute. Keep it casual and under 75 words.
5
Create a List of FAQs for My Business
Use to build a website FAQ section or prepare for sales calls.
Copy and paste this prompt
I run a [type of business] in [city]. Generate a list of 8 frequently asked questions that my potential customers are likely to have, along with clear, helpful answers. Write the answers in a friendly, confident tone, like an experienced owner who has heard it all.
6
Write a Nextdoor or Facebook Post to Get Local Leads
Use to promote your services in local community groups.
Copy and paste this prompt
Write a short post I can share in a local Facebook group or on Nextdoor to let neighbors know about my [type of business]. I am based in [city/neighborhood]. Make it friendly and helpful, not salesy. Mention that I am local, available, and happy to give free quotes. Under 100 words.
7
Summarize a Long Email Thread
Use when a customer conversation has gone back and forth and you need to catch up fast.
Copy and paste this prompt
Here is an email thread with a customer: [paste thread]. Summarize the key points in 3-5 bullet points: what they want, what has been agreed to, any open questions, and what the next step should be.
8
Write a Simple Scope of Work
Use before starting a job to set clear expectations with a customer.
Copy and paste this prompt
Write a simple one-page scope of work for a [type of job] I am doing for a customer. The work includes [describe tasks]. The total cost is $[amount]. Payment terms are [terms]. Include a section for both of us to sign and date. Keep the language plain and professional, not full of legal jargon.
9
Brainstorm Ways to Get More Referrals
Use when word-of-mouth has slowed down and you want fresh ideas.
Copy and paste this prompt
I run a [type of business] and most of my customers come from referrals. Give me 8 specific, practical ideas to get more referrals from my existing customers. Focus on things a solo operator or small team can actually do without a big marketing budget.
10
Write a Monthly Newsletter for Customers
Use to stay top-of-mind with past clients and generate repeat business.
Copy and paste this prompt
Write a short, friendly email newsletter I can send to my past customers this month. My business is [type of business] in [city]. Include a seasonal tip or reminder relevant to [month], a brief update about my business, and a soft call to action encouraging them to reach out if they need anything. Keep it under 200 words and conversational.
